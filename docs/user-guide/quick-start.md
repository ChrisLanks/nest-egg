# Quick Start — Nest Egg in 5 minutes

New to Nest Egg? This gets you up and running fast. No finance knowledge needed.

---

## 1. Create your account

Go to the app and click **Sign Up**. Enter your email and choose a password
(at least 12 characters — the app will show you what's needed).

After signing up, check your email for a verification link and click it.

---

## 2. Tell us what you want to work on

You'll be asked: **"What brings you here?"** Pick the one that fits:

- **Track my spending** — You want to know where your money goes and set a budget
- **Plan for retirement** — You want to know when you could stop working
- **Understand my investments** — You have a 401(k) or brokerage account and want to see what's in it

Don't worry about picking the "right" one — you can explore everything later.

---

## 3. Connect a bank account (recommended)

Connecting a bank account lets Nest Egg automatically import your transactions
and track your balances. This is the fastest way to see useful information.

**Is this safe?** Yes. The connection goes through Plaid — the same service
used by Venmo and Coinbase. You log in on your bank's own page. Nest Egg never
sees your password and can only read (not move) your money.

Prefer to skip it? You can add accounts manually later from the Accounts page.

---

## 4. Set one budget

Once you're in the app, create one budget for a spending category you care
about — for example, Groceries or Dining Out.

A **budget** is just a monthly spending limit. Example: "I want to spend no
more than $400 on groceries this month." Nest Egg tracks your progress in real
time.

---

## 5. Check your net worth

Your **net worth** = everything you own − everything you owe.

Example: $10,500 in savings − $6,200 car loan = $4,300 net worth.

It doesn't matter if the number is low right now. What matters is whether it's
growing over time. Nest Egg charts it for you automatically.

---
